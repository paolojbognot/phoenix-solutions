Our Story

On September 10, 2018, a daring venture was started at Pelican Bay State Prison to teach prisoners computer programming. It focused on helping prisoners build relevant skills in technology and other areas so that they can more easily transition to productive employment once they are out of prison. It was called "Code.7370" and was co-sponsored by The Last Mile.

The very first class of men were dubbed class "Beta" as a nod to the last preview build of an application before it hits the world. Class "Beta" started with 15 men and underwent a grueling six months of technical training in a variety of computer programming languages such as HTML, CSS, and Javascript. In addition, they learned to harness popular web libraries such as jQuery, Twitter's Bootstrap and Handlebars in order to design and develope modern websites that meet the current web standards.
